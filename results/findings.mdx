The asymmetry suggests:

- The model identifies tool-call by the **absence of no-tool features** rather than presence of dedicated tool features
- This may reflect the SAE basis not being optimized for this specific task

## Causal SAE Feature Patching

We tested whether the top 20 SAE features are **causally important** by zeroing each individually.

### Results

<Card title="Individual Feature Effects" icon="circle-exclamation">
  **All 20 features produce ~0.000 change** in model logit difference when patched out individually.
  
  - Mean baseline logit diff: **-0.323**
  - Per-feature interventions: remain near baseline after rounding
  - Largest correlational features (d > 19) are **causally inert**
</Card>

### Positive Controls

To verify the intervention pathway works:

| Intervention | Mean Logit Shift | Interpretation |
|--------------|------------------|----------------|
| Ablate L2H1 (known causal) | -0.065 | Modest effect, as expected |
| Strong control at SAE hook | 4.159 (mean abs) | Pathway can move logits |
| Individual SAE features | ~0.000 | Effects are distributed |

<Warning>
  **Key finding**: Features with enormous observational effect sizes (Cohen's d > 19) can be completely causally inert. 
  
  **Lesson**: Correlation-based interpretability methods (including standard SAE feature analysis) can produce deeply misleading results. **Causal interventions are non-negotiable** for mechanistic claims.
</Warning>

## Adversarial Probing

We tested 50 adversarial examples designed to break surface-level strategies.

### Results by Category

| Category | Description | Accuracy | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **Numbers, no tool** | Static facts with numbers | **73.3%** (11/15) | "What year was the Eiffel Tower built?" |

## Summary

The detailed findings reveal:

1. **Distributed importance**: No single head dominates; top scores within 8%
2. **Compute → readout dissociation**: Patching and DLA identify different heads
3. **SAE features are epiphenomenal**: Enormous correlational signals (d > 19) have zero causal effect individually
4. **Circuit is redundant**: Not sufficient (50%) and near-non-necessary (100%)
5. **Unified tool-type circuit**: 8-10/10 head overlap across all tool types
6. **Robust rankings**: Spearman 0.995 correlation across seeds
7. **Genuine but imperfect reasoning**: 100% on tools-without-numbers, but 73.3% on numbers-without-tools
