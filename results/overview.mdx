## Fine-Tuning Performance

The GPT-2 Small model achieved near-perfect accuracy on the adversarially balanced tool-calling dataset:

  </Card>
  <Card title="Tool-Call Accuracy" icon="wrench">

  <Card title="No-Tool Accuracy" icon="book">
    **98.98%** (485/490 correct)

  <Card title="Mean Confidence" icon="bullseye">
    **99.97%** on correct predictions

The 5 errors are all false positives (no-tool examples misclassified as tool-call), with low confidence (mean 1.87%). This suggests genuinely ambiguous cases at the decision boundary rather than systematic failures.

<Info>
  The high accuracy indicates that GPT-2 Small can learn the tool-call/no-tool distinction even when obvious surface features (numbers, dynamic-cue words) are balanced across classes.
</Info>

## Key Findings

### 1. Distributed Circuit Structure

**The circuit is highly distributed and redundant** — fundamentally different from the original unbalanced dataset analysis:

- **No single dominant head**: Top 3 heads (L7H6, L2H1, L6H2) are within 8% of each other
- **Flat importance distribution**: Max patching score is only 0.606, vs 4.28 in the original analysis
- **Top heads span multiple layers**: L2 (early), L4-L7 (mid), L9-L11 (late)

| Head | Patching Score | Role |
|------|----------------|------|
| **L7H6** | **0.606** | Primary mid-layer computation |
| **L2H1** | **0.592** | Early feature extraction |
| **L6H2** | **0.559** | Mid-layer computation |
| L4H2 | 0.384 | Mid-layer support |
| L10H4 | 0.351 | Late readout |

<Warning>
  The identified circuit is **not sufficient and near-non-necessary** for the tool-calling decision. This contrasts sharply with the original analysis (necessity was 76.7%). The mechanism is **highly distributed and redundant**.
</Warning>

### 3. Impact of Confound Balancing

**L6H4, the originally dominant head, became irrelevant** when numbers were balanced:

- **Original analysis**: L6H4 scored 4.28 (3× higher than any other head)
- **Balanced dataset**: L6H4 patching score ≈ 0 (outside top 15)
- **Interpretation**: L6H4's original dominance was driven by number-detection shortcuts

When numbers appear equally in both classes, L6H4 stops attending to them and becomes largely inert. This is strong evidence that **the circuits we find depend on the confounds we allow**.

### 4. Compute → Readout Pattern Persists

The two-stage processing pattern remains robust:

**Stage 1: Early/Mid-Network Computation (L2–L7)**
- Heads process input and write "this needs a tool" signal
- High patching importance, low DLA scores
- Examples: L2H1, L6H2, L7H6

**Stage 2: Late-Network Readout (L9–L11)**
- Heads read the signal and translate to logit differences
- Low patching importance, high DLA scores
- Examples: L11H8 (DLA: 13.43), L10H5 (DLA: 6.36), L11H7 (DLA: 5.23)

### 5. Unified Tool-Type Circuit

The model uses **a single circuit for all tool types**:

| Tool Type | Top Head | Top-10 Overlap with Calculator |
|-----------|----------|--------------------------------|
| Calculator | L2H1 (0.94) | — |
| Lookup | L2H1 (1.44) | 9/10 |
| Datetime | L2H1 (0.79) | 9/10 |
| Unit Conversion | L2H1 (1.98) | 10/10 |

L2H1 dominates across 3 of 4 tool types, suggesting it performs general "needs external processing" detection that is largely tool-type-agnostic.

## Circuit Size Analysis

A circuit-size sweep (100 balanced cases) revealed that behavior is recovered only with large fractions of heads:

| Heads Kept | Sufficiency | Necessity |
|------------|-------------|------------|
| 1–30 heads | 50% | 100% |
| 60 heads | 86% | 55% |
| 100 heads | 99% | 50% |
| 140 heads | 100% | 50% |

<Tip>
  **Interpretation**: The mechanism appears distributed/redundant — performance is recovered only when large fractions of heads are retained. Many overlapping subsets can independently reconstruct the decision.
</Tip>

## Training Dynamics

The circuit exhibits **phase-transition-like emergence**:

- **Step 200**: 60% accuracy, all patching scores ≈ 0
- **Step 300**: 96% accuracy, multiple heads activate simultaneously
- **Circuit snaps into place** within a single 100-step window
- **L2H1 dominates from the start** once the circuit appears
- **L6H4 remains marginal** throughout training (never exceeds 0.05)

## What This Means

The adversarial balancing reveals a critical insight:

<Card title="Key Takeaway" icon="lightbulb">
  **The circuits we find depend on the confounds we allow.** A clean, localized circuit (L6H4-dominated) emerged when surface features correlated with the label. Removing those correlations didn't destroy the model's ability to learn the task (99.5% accuracy), but it did destroy the clean circuit.
</Card>

The resulting mechanism is distributed, redundant, and harder to characterize — arguably more representative of how real-world models process complex decisions.
